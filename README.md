## What This Is

The Human-AI Triangulation (HAT) Framework is a structured methodology for human-directed AI collaboration that prioritizes evidence classification, adversarial review, and human authority retention over AI consensus.

HAT was not designed in theory. It was extracted from operational practice — research projects where the failure modes of unstructured AI collaboration became visible, and where governance architecture was built in response to those failures.

The core principle: **AI systems should function as instruments of investigation, not as judges of operators.**

## Core Framework Principles

**Evidence gates over content filters.**  
Claims require supporting evidence before advancing from hypothesis to working assumption. Procedural gates cannot be evaded linguistically; content filters consistently can.

**Operator-declared context over inferred mode.**  
User-controlled framing is more reliable and more accountable than AI mode inference.

**Instrument design over judge design.**  
AI systems should reveal information, surface assumptions, and map uncertainty — not evaluate operators or adjudicate truth.

**Adversarial structure in multi-AI workflows.**  
Filtration requires at least one agent explicitly assigned to falsification. Consensus between AI agents is not independent confirmation.

**Human authority retention.**  
The operator’s highest-value function is often restraint — shelving, delaying, refusing advancement. The human is the governor. The AI is the engine.

## Governance Report — Scope

The white paper addresses six domains:

1. Stewardship Failures in AI development and deployment
1. Operator Mode Detection — inference reliability and misclassification risk
1. Sycophancy and Amplification — confirmation loops, narrative drift, dependency
1. Triangulation Systems — amplification vs. filtration design
1. AI as Judge vs. AI as Instrument
1. Human Adaptation and Adversarial Use

All findings are classified by evidence status:  
🟢 Supported · 🟡 Under Investigation · 🟠 Speculative · 🔴 Unsupported · ⚫ Unknown

**Research Development:** Human-directed synthesis supported by multiple AI systems operating under adversarial triangulation and evidence-review procedures.

## Field Case

The JIREH HAT Pilot Report is an operator account of a multi-phase research project governed under HAT methodology. It documents mode misclassification, narrative drift detection, preservation-without-endorsement workflows, and one complete procedural evidence-gate cycle. It is included as a field validation artifact, not as a research source.
